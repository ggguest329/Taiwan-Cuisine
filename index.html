Taiwanese cuisine

Small eats, and a lot of them, are the big thing in Taiwan.

Taiwanese cuisine itself is often associated with influences from mid to southern provinces of China.
Chinese food can be found in Taiwan, alongside Fujian and Hakka-style as well as native Taiwanese dishes.

Ingredients and culture

  1. Pork, seafood, chicken, rice, and soy are very common ingredients
  2. Taiwan's cuisine has also been influenced by its geographic location, seafood figures prominently in their cuisine.
  3. An important part of Taiwanese cuisine are xiaochi, substantial snacks along the lines of Spanish tapas or Levantine meze.

Desserts

  • Baobing - a Chinese shaved ice dessert very common in China, Taiwan, Malaysia, and Vietnam.
  • Bubble tea, also known as pearl milk tea - chewy tapioca balls added to milk tea.
  • Grass jelly – (Mesona procumbens) Served hot or cold.
